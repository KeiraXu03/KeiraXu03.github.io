Jigsaw puzzle solving requires understanding both local fragment details and global
spatial relationships. While most traditional approaches focus only on visual cues
such as edge matching, we propose a vision–language framework that leverages textual
context to enhance puzzle assembly — especially for challenging **eroded-gap** puzzles.

At its core is the **Vision-Language Hierarchical Semantic Alignment (VLHSA)** module,
which aligns visual patches with textual descriptions through multi-level semantic
matching from local tokens to global context. The module integrates a multimodal
architecture combining **dual visual encoders** with language features for cross-modal
reasoning. Experiments show significant improvements over state-of-the-art models
across datasets, including a **14.2 percentage-point gain in piece accuracy**, and
ablations confirm the critical role of the VLHSA module over vision-only baselines.

**Authors:** Zhuoning Xu, Xinyan Liu
